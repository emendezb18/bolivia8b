Bolivia historical events

1.pre-colonization indigeneous people - 110 AD-1500

A major Indian civilization developed at the southern end of Lake Titicaca. The Tiwanaku (Tiahuanaco) Indians built gigantic monuments and carved statues. Their civilization declined rapidly during the thirteenth century. and was substituted by the Aymaras Is in 1450 when the Incas started and fight of power with the aymaras. The Incas won the battle but however failed to conquer the nomadic tribes in the eastern Bolivian lowlands.

2. colonization of bolivia - 1538

In the sixteenth century, Spanish conquistadors from Cuzco and Asunción took control of the area. Upper Peru was the name given to Bolivia during the majority of the Spanish colonial era, and it was governed by the Royal Audiencia of Charcas

3. independence of bolivia - 8/6/1825

On August 6, 1825, Bolivia proclaimed its independence from Spain. By appointing James B. Thornton as Chargé d'Affaires on March 16, 1837, the United States recognized the Peru-Bolivian Confederation. Thornton was assigned to Peru but was welcomed by the Confederation of Peru and Bolivia

4. bolivian national revolution - 1952

Bolivia's defeat by Paraguay in the Chaco War (1932-1935) marked a turning point for both Bolivia and the Bolivian people. The Nationalist Revolutionary Movement (MNR) emerged as a broadly based party in opposition to the status quo. Among their leftist policies were agrarian reform, nationalization of the mining sector, adult suffrage and a focus on rural health and education. the Revolution was considered one of the three most important social revolutions in Latin America

5. evo morales elected 2006

Evo Morales was the first Bolivian president since 1982 to win a majority of the national vote. He controversially redistributed 134 million acres of land from state or private ownership to Indigenous families. The countrys GDP grew an average of 4.8% a year from 2004 to 2017 under his leadership. The population living in big poverty halved from approximately 36% down to 17% during that time. he made a big impact in bolivia although he has a polemic reputation
