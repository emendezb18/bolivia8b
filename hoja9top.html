9.Madidi National Park

Bolivia's upper Amazon river basin contains a national park called Madidi. It was founded in 1995 and covers 18,958 km2. Madidi is a part of one of the biggest protected areas in the world, along with the nearby reserves Manuripi-Heath, Apolobamba, and the Manu Biosphere Reserve. One of the biggest protected places in the world is Madidi National Park. The park's elevation ranges from 200 to 6,000 meters (or 1,968 to 19,685 feet) above sea level. Visitors can expect to see steep hills, running rivers and streams, lush, deep rainforests, cloud forests, and snow-capped mountains. Here is a list of animals you might find there:

  1. Spectacled Bear (Tremarctos ornatus)
  2. Jaguar (Panthera onca)
  3. Madidi Titi (Callicebus aureipalatii)
  4. Ocelot (Felis pardalis)
  5. Giant Otter (Pteronura brasiliensis)
  6. Andean Cock-of-the-Rock (Rupicola peruviana)
  7. White-Lipped Peccary (Tayassu pecari albirostris)
  8. Pink River Dolphin (Inia geoffrensis)
