7. Salar de Uyuni

The Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia is considered as one of the most unique and wild landscapes on Earth. The largest salt flat in the world, covering more than 4,050 square miles of the Altiplano, is what remains of ancient lakes that long ago drained. In this location, a thick layer of salt covers the horizon and rises from the ground in quilted, polygonal patterns. It is a popular vacation spot for travelers doing road trips and is home to the first salt hotel in the world. It might be a logistical nightmare or an amazing experience due to the raw beauty and desolation.
