5. The Andes mountains

The Andes are made up of a large chain of incredibly high plateaus that are surrounded by even higher peaks. The tallest peaks in the Western Hemisphere are found in the Andes. On the border of Chile and Argentina, Mount Aconcagua is the tallest of them all at 22,831 feet . The Andes are a sequence of parallel and transverse mountain ranges, or cordilleras, rather than a single line of frightening peaks. The chain includes distinct eastern and western ranges with the names Cordillera Oriental and Cordilleran Occidental, respectively. Some historians claim that the term Andes is taken from the Quechuan word anti, while others say that it comes from the word anta. Maybe it makes more sense to connect it to the word anta from the earlier Aymara language, which generally refers to the color copper.
