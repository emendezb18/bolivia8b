8. Jesuit Missions of Chiquitos

The Jesuit Missions of Chiquitos are a group of six churches that stand in for the ideal, theocratic towns that made up the reductions. The style of the churches is unique; they are massive buildings, made mainly of wood, with a double layer roof and a huge porch roof. In the year 1540, Saint Ignatius Loyola and his companions organized the Society of Jesus. The goal of the Jesuit mission is to bring about the reconciliation of both men and women with God, with themselves, with one another, and with God's creation.
